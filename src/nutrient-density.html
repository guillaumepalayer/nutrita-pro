Nutrient dense vs Nutrient poor foods

Nutrient dense vs Nutrient poor foods

Highly nutrient dense foods have the required amounts, full range and proper ratios of vitamins, minerals, essential fatty acids and essential amino acids necessary for supporting basic health. Nutrient poor foods don’t.

Who is it for?

Who is it for?

Everyone should pay attention to the Nutrient density score. This is true regardless of your age, gender, health status or personal goals.

Although individual requirements may vary, eating a diet that is nutritionally complete and sufficiently nutrient dense is essential for being healthy and performing well.

How does it work?

How does it work?

Our Nutrient density score is pretty advanced compared to what’s out there. It takes into account differences between animal and plant foods. It factors in how much of certain nutrients you’re likely to absorb and how much of the active form of a nutrient you’re likely to get.

Our score does more than just count up nutrients in food - it gets to the heart of the matter by answering the question How nourishing is this food?
